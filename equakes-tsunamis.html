Earthquakes

Definition: A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves caused by the sudden displacement of water from events like earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.

Common Causes:

  1. Earthquakes: Most common, from the ocean floor rising or sinking due to tectonic movement.
  2. Landslides: Can trigger tsunamis with large amounts of debris.
  3. Volcanic activity: Eruptions can cause tsunamis.
  4. Weather: Some weather conditions may trigger tsunamis.
  5. Near-Earth objects: Asteroids or comets hitting the ocean can cause tsunamis.

Survival Tips

Higher Ground: You should quickly evacuate to higher elevation or inland, away from the coast.

Staying Informed: Monitor alerts and warnings via radio, phone apps, or local authorities.

Avoiding Water: Do not return to the beach or enter the water until authorities declare it safe.
