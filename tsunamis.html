Tsunamis

Information

Definition A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves caused by the sudden displacement of water from events like earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.

Formation

  1. Earthquakes: Most common, from the ocean floor rising or sinking due to tectonic movement.
  2. Landslides: Can trigger tsunamis with large amounts of debris.
  3. Volcanic activity: Eruptions can cause tsunamis.
  4. Weather: Some weather conditions may trigger tsunamis.
  5. Near-Earth objects: Asteroids or comets hitting the ocean can cause tsunamis.

Categories Tsunamis are categorized by a new Tsunami Intensity Scale. This scale has 12 points, depending on how felt it is and the damage it causes.

Safety Guidelines

Before a Tsunami If there is an earthquake first, protect yourself before doing anything else. Evacuate and get as inland and as high as possible. Listen to emergency alerts and follow evacuation orders. Have an emergency plan in case of a tsunami.

During a Tsunami Immediately move to a safe place (higher ground + inland) if there are signs of a tsunami. Listen to officials and emergency alerts. If you are in the water, grab onto something that floats. If you are in a boat, head out to sea in the direction of the waves. If you are closer to a harbor, however, go inland.

After a Tsunami Monitor alerts and warnings via radio, phone apps, or local authorities. Do not return to the beach or enter the water until authorities declare it safe. Save phone calls and your phone battery as infrastructure may be damaged. Be cautious of this damaged infrastructure and possible debris.
