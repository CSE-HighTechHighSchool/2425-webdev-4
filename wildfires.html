Wildfires

Information

Definition A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire that spreads rapidly through vegetation, often in rural areas, forests, grasslands, or other natural landscapes, driven by wind and dry conditions.

Formation

  1. Lightning strikes
  2. Human activities (campfires, discarded cigarettes, fireworks)
  3. Electrical equipment malfunctions (e.g., power lines)
  4. Arson

Categories Wildfires are classified by their location in the forest they are forming in. The categories are: Ground, Surface, Crown, Spotting. Ground wildfires burn underground, surface fires at the low levels of the forest, crown fires burn at the upper levels of forests (in the upper canopy), and spotting is when burning particles of a fire is carried by the wind to another location.

Safety Guidelines

Before a Wildfire Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, and medicines. Know your evacuation routes and evacuate early if advised by authorities. Keep easily flammable items away from any potential sources of fire.

During a Wildfire Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation. Use a mask or cloth to cover your nose and mouth to filter out smoke. Stay informed via radio or official channels for updates on evacuation routes and safety measures.

After a Wildfire Check with officials to make sure it is safe to return to your house. Conserve phone battery for emergencies. Avoid damaged areas and be cautious of fallen power lines or structural damage. Wear appropriate protective clothing during cleanup. Check for embers and sparks that could start another fire and stomp them out.
