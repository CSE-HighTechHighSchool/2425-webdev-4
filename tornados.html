Tornadoes

Information

Definition Tornadoes are violently rotating columns of air that form at the base of a thunderstorm and extend to the ground. They become visible when condensation funnel forms, consisting of water droplets, dust, and debris.

Formation Atmospheric instability triggers tornado formation when warm, moist air near the ground combines with cooler dry air. Wind shear, including changes in wind speed and direction with height, promotes rotation and tornado development.

Categories Tornadoes are classified using the Fujita Tornado Damage Scale, ranging from F0 to F5, based on wind speed and resulting damage.

Safety Guidelines

Before the Storm Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, and medicines. Know your evacuation routes and identify safe spaces in your house. Secure outdoor items and install storm shutters if possible.

During the Storm Stay indoors and away from windows. Move to the lowest, most sturdy room in your house. Follow evacuation orders if issued and stay tuned to radio/alerts for updates.

After the Storm Conserve phone battery for emergencies. Avoid damaged areas and be cautious of fallen power lines or structural damage. Wear appropriate protective clothing during cleanup.
