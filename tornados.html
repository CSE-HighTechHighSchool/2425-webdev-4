Tornadoes

Information
Tornadoes Definition: Tornadoes are violently rotating columns of air that form at the base of a thunderstorm and go to the ground. They are visible only if a condensation funnel is formed. These are made up of water droplets, dust, and debris.

Formation: Atmospheric instability leads to the formation of tornadoes. Warm, moist air near the ground can be combined with cooler dry air and wind shear. This, along with a change in wind speed and/or direction with height, can promote rotation and form a tornado.

Categories: Tornadoes are categorized by wind speed using the Fujita Tornado Damage Scale (Category F0-F5).
Safety
Safety Before the storm: Create an emergency kit (food, water, medicines). Know your evacuation routes or have safe spaces in your house. Secure your home (shutters, bring in outdoor items). Be prepared for emergencies, and make sure everyone knows how to deal with them.

During the storm: Stay indoors, away from windows. Go to the lowest and most study room in your house. Follow evacuation orders if issued. Use radio/alerts for updates.

After the storm: Save your phone battery for emergecnies. Avoid damaged areas. Check for power lines or structural damage. Wear appropriate clothes when cleaning up.
