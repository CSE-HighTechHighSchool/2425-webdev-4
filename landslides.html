Landslides

Information

Definition Landslides are the downward movement of rock, soil, and organic matter on a slope due to gravity. They can be triggered by factors such as heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, or human activities like deforestation.

Types of Landslides Landslides vary in type and speed, including rockfalls, debris flows, and slow-moving earth slides. Each type poses unique hazards depending on the geography and conditions of the area.

Causes Landslides are often caused by natural forces, including heavy rains, rapid snowmelt, and earthquakes, which weaken slopes. Human activities, like road construction and mining, can also destabilize land, increasing the likelihood of landslides.

Safety Guidelines

Before a Landslide Stay informed about areas prone to landslides and watch for warning signs such as cracks in walls, tilting trees, or unusual sounds of rocks or soil movement. Prepare an emergency kit and develop an evacuation plan for rapid action.

During a Landslide If indoors, take shelter under sturdy furniture. If outdoors, move quickly to higher ground and avoid valleys or low-lying areas. Listen to emergency alerts and follow evacuation orders if issued.

After a Landslide Avoid the affected area to reduce the risk of secondary slides. Stay alert for flooding or structural damage, and report any downed power lines or broken utility connections to authorities.
