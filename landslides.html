Landslides

Information

Definition Landslides are the downward movement of rock, soil, and organic matter on a slope due to gravity.

Types of Landslides Landslides vary in type and speed, including rockfalls, debris flows, and slow-moving earth slides.

Causes Landslides are often caused by natural forces such as rain, snowmelt, or earthquakes. Human activities, like road construction and mining, can also destabilize land.

Safety Guidelines

Before a Landslide Stay informed about areas prone to landslides and prepare an emergency kit and evacuation plan.

During a Landslide Take shelter under sturdy furniture if indoors, or move to higher ground if outdoors. Follow emergency alerts and evacuation orders.

After a Landslide Avoid the affected area, stay alert for secondary slides, and report any structural damage to authorities.
