Hurricanes

Information

Definition Hurricanes are intense tropical storms characterized by strong winds of 74 mph or higher, heavy rainfall, and dangerous storm surges. These elements combine to create potentially devastating impacts on coastal and inland areas alike.

Formation Hurricanes develop over warm ocean waters in tropical regions, where they draw energy from the heat and moisture. As they travel, these powerful storms intensify, growing stronger with each mile across the warm seas.

Categories Hurricanes are classified by wind speed on the Saffir-Simpson scale, which ranges from Category 1 to Category 5. Category 5 hurricanes are the most severe, featuring extreme winds capable of causing catastrophic damage.

Safety Guidelines

Before the Storm Before a hurricane, prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, and medications. Familiarize yourself with evacuation routes, and secure your home by installing shutters and bringing in any outdoor items that could become hazards.

During the Storm During the storm, stay indoors and keep away from windows to avoid flying debris. Follow any evacuation orders immediately if they’re issued, and use emergency alerts to stay informed about the storm’s progress and safety updates.

After the Storm Avoid floodwaters and damaged areas. Check for power lines or structural damage.
